**Languages:** [[Common]], [[Persemon (Language)|Persemon]] ([[Primordial]] Based)

**Pers** is a [[Nations|nation]] located within the [[Southern Continent]] of [[Mayl]]. It shares a border with the [[Ghunal Tribes]] and [[The Krystad Dominion]] to the north. Pers is considered the remnant Shahdom of the [[Persemon Empire]], which was situated to the east of the [[Ghunal Desert]]. Currently, Pers is run by nomadic warlords, who master over peasants who farm crops for them. 

### Meta Rambling
This is a land of steppes, rolling hills of grass, with lots of sheep cows, and of course horses. This is supposed to be a weird mix between iranian and nomadic turkic steppe culture. 

History wise, I want the Persemon Empire to originate within the rocky and rugged eastern area in the north of the current Pers and south east Ghunal Desert, expanded far south through the steppes. This empire sort of "tamed" the rugged nomadic warlords. They built some cities to defend against the groups of horsemen that would fight against them. 

The Persemons resided in the river valley in northern Pers. This area is rocky and rough, but was fertile enough to create a strong civilization and empire.

These were large cities of sandstone that they would import from their homeland. Eventually this empire would fall, and would leave the steppes in a power vacuum. Some warlords attempted to call themselves shah, and the successor of the Persemon Empire. Eventually one [[Shah Dimurlan Bey]] united the warlords under the [[First Pers Horde]]. This horde would conquer much of the southern kingdoms up to the [[Piri Coast]] and [[Sviddian Desert]] to the west, and [[Horgo]] to the south.

They had a hard time crossing the mountains into Horgo, and could not make their way through the Sviddian desert to conquer further westward. 

They had a hard time conquering the wooded kingdoms to the south of them, but were able to employ a guerilla tactics to destroy supply lines, and ambush armies. Eventually the kingdoms fell and were forced to pay tribute to Dimurlan. 

Once Dimurlan died, the horde fell apart, and most of the Persites returned back home to their steppes. The raiding and war left the woodland kingdoms to the south in ruin. From there, many factions fought to gather power from the vacuum.
